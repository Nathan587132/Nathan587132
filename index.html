Goku and Wukong

All about Goku

Goku, inspired by Sun Wukong, was born on Planet Vegeta. He is a Saiyan, an alien species resembling humans but with a monkey-like tail. This tail allows them to transform into a Great Ape during a full moon.

Throughout his life, Goku has lost his tail three times, but when he transforms into Super Saiyan 4, he resembles a monkey once again.

Goku is known for his enormous appetite, eating an entire week's worth of food in a single sitting, especially when powering up into forms like Super Saiyan, which requires vast amounts of energy.

All about Wukong

Sun Wukong, also known as the Monkey King, was born in the Mountain of Flowers and Fruit. He is a legendary character from the 16th-century Chinese novel "Journey to the West." Wukong is a monkey endowed with human-like attributes, such as intelligence, strength, and agility.

His golden eyes have the ability to penetrate illusions and see malevolent beings, while his body is diamond-hard and impervious to most conventional weapons.

Wukong is also known for his ability to create clones of himself from his hair and possesses magical powers that make him nearly invincible.

The Buddha arranged for Wukong to be fed iron pellets when he was hungry and molten copper when he was thirsty, as part of his divine trials.

Who is Stronger?

Wukong, as a Vastayan trickster, uses his incredible strength, agility, and intelligence to confuse his opponents and outmaneuver them in battle. His cunning is enhanced by his magical abilities, such as shape-shifting and creating clones.

On the other hand, Goku possesses Superhuman Strength, Speed, Durability, and Endurance. Additionally, Goku has developed Ultra Instinct, a divine ability that allows him to perceive and react to attacks before they happen, making him one of the most powerful beings in the Dragon Ball universe.
